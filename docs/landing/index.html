Features

Everything your phone does, now on the desktop.

One app on each side, native on both — the same things, in both directions.

Screen mirroring

Two screens. Either direction.

Hardware-encoded H.264 or HEVC, decoded with VideoToolbox, rendered with no jitter buffer — latency you stop noticing.

Phone in a window

Mirror Android into a Mac window and tap-to-click with the mouse.

Drive the Mac from the couch

The Mac's screen on your phone — cursor, scroll, right-click, keyboard.

Pocket second display

The phone becomes an extra monitor shaped to its own aspect ratio.

File browser

Your whole phone, in a window.

Browse the entire storage Finder-style — real thumbnails, breadcrumbs, one-click downloads. Drag a file from Finder into any open folder and it lands right there on the phone.

Full storage access Thumbnails Drag & drop upload Wi-Fi speed
Messages

Texts, on a real keyboard.

Read every SMS conversation on the Mac and reply from there. Contact names resolved, short codes detected and kept reply-safe.

Full chat UI Send replies Contact names
Quick Drop

Files fall out of the notch.

Press a global hotkey anywhere on the Mac — a drop zone slides down from the notch. Drop a file or folder and it is on your phone. Incoming files appear the same way, with one-click accept.

Global hotkey Accept / reject consent Live progress island

Clipboard that follows you

Copy on the phone, paste on the Mac — and back. Or select text in any Android app and hit “Send to Mac”.

phone · copied

Photo gallery

Browse the phone's photo library from the Mac, open a full-screen viewer with zoom, and download originals in full resolution.

Live system monitor

The phone shows your Mac's wallpaper with live CPU, RAM, disk and battery. The Mac mirrors it back with the phone's vitals.

Multi-device

Pair multiple phones with one Mac, or one phone with multiple Macs. Each pairing keeps its own keys.

Security & privacy

Your Wi-Fi is the whole internet it needs.

No relay servers, no accounts, no telemetry. Unplug your router from the world — AirBridge keeps working.

Ed25519 key pairs

Each device mints a cryptographic identity on first launch. Pairing is a one-time QR scan that swaps public keys.

Signed reconnections

Every reconnect is verified with a signed timestamp, and the video channel is gated by a key-derived token.

Consent before every transfer

Files only move after you tap Accept on the receiving device. SHA-256 checksums verify what arrived.

Auditable, MIT-licensed

Every line of both apps is public on GitHub. Read it, build it, fork it.

Setup

Pair once. Forget it exists.

The Mac advertises itself over Bonjour, the phone finds it on its own. No IP addresses, ever.

1

Show a QR code

On the Mac: Settings, then Add New Device. A QR code appears.

2

Scan it

Point the phone's camera at it. Keys exchanged — that's the whole ceremony.

3

Walk away

The devices find each other on any shared Wi-Fi and reconnect on their own.
